    <h3>Proteomics Facility</h3>

    <div class="imagemodule float-left col-md-6">
        <div class="inner">
            <figure class="image"><span class="imagewrapper"><img alt="" data-id="0"
                        src="https://dktk.dkfz.de/download_file/view/78415bde-f4a8-4ca1-aa49-cc9338bb6647" /></span>

                <figcaption class="smalltext"><span class="copyright"><span class="copysign">&copy;</span> Bernhard
                        K&uuml;ster/ Technische
                        Universit&auml;t M&uuml;nchen</span></figcaption>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>

    <p><strong>The Core Facility Proteomics at the DKTK Partner site comprises seven high-end mass
            spectrometers, several liquid chromatography systems and terabyte scale computing
            infrastructure.</strong></p>

    <p>The range of supported applications span quantitative protein expression profiling of human
        and animal tissues, tumors, cell lines and body fluids, dynamic analysis of
        post-translational modifications, protein-protein interactions, drug target identification,
        drug mechanism of action analysis and many more. We can offer discovery type proteomic
        experiments using multidimensional chromatography coupled to the latest Orbitrap technology
        as well as targeted assays using parallel reaction monitoring. All major quantification
        workflows are established in the laboratory including SILAC, TMT and a range of label-free
        analysis. We typically work on the basis of scientific collaboration in which we engage our
        expertise along the entire path from experiment design through to data acquisition and
        bioinformatic analysis.</p>

    <h3>IBioTUM-Tissue</h3>

    <div class="imagemodule float-right col-md-6">
        <div class="inner">
            <figure class="image"><span class="imagewrapper"><img alt="kfbfotografie.jpg" data-id="879"
                        src="https://dktk.dkfz.de/download_file/view_inline/972aa761-44ec-4015-a014-904957fff120" /></span>

                <figcaption class="smalltext"><span class="copyright"><span class="copysign">&copy;</span>
                        kfbfotografie</span></figcaption>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>

    <p>The tissue bank of the Klinikum rechts der Isar and the Technical University of Munich has
        been systematically storing fresh, unfixed tumor tissue and corresponding non-neoplastic
        tissue since 1989. The tissue bank currently has an extensive tissue collection of both
        malignant tumor tissue and non-neoplastic tissue as well as benign tumors or tumor
        precursors.<br />
        <br />
        The tissue bank offers a wide range of services, including
    </p>

    <ul>
        <li>Long-term storage of samples in special facilities at -80&deg;C, -20&deg;C and in the
            gas phase of liquid nitrogen.</li>
        <li>Advice and support in the selection of methods and patient collectives.</li>
        <li>Preparation of tissue sections from cryosamples and formalin-fixed block preparations.
        </li>
        <li>Histological H&amp;E diagnostics.</li>
        <li>Planning and production of tissue microarrays (TMAs).</li>
        <li>Maintaining a comprehensive and secure database and providing detailed information on
            samples and processing history.</li>
        <li>Scanning of slides.</li>
        <li>Immunohistochemistry.</li>
    </ul>

    <p>The tissue bank collaborates with other institutions and departments, such as the routine
        laboratory of the Institute of Pathology and Pathological Anatomy at the Technical
        University of Munich, as well as with Comparative Experimental Pathology (CeP) and is
        actively involved in networks such as the German Biobank Node (GBN) and the European biobank
        network BBMRI-ERIC. Through these activities, the tissue bank contributes to the further
        development and quality assurance of biobanks.</p>

    <p>For more information, please click <a href="https://web.med.tum.de/path/gewebebank/" target="_blank">here</a>.
    </p>

    <h3>Xenograft mouse model of acute leukemias</h3>

    <p><strong>The Core Facility &ldquo;Xenograft mouse model of acute leukemias&rdquo; studies
            primary patients&acute; tumor cells growing in mice, performs preclinical treatment
            trials and molecular studies on basic disease biology.</strong></p>

    <div class="imagemodule float-right col-md-3">
        <div class="inner">
            <figure class="image"><span class="imagewrapper"><img alt="" data-id="0"
                        src="https://dktk.dkfz.de/download_file/view/5faaab05-946b-46b8-8995-a1a666bb3400" /></span>

                <figcaption class="smalltext"><span class="caption-text">In vivo bioluminescence
                        imaging (BLI) allows reliable and sensitive follow-up of preclinical
                        treatment trials</span> <span class="copyright"><span class="copysign">
                            &copy;</span> AG Jeremias</span></figcaption>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>

    <p>The core facility offers:</p>

    <ul class="dk-custom-list">
        <li>engrafting and amplifying primary tumor cells from patients with ALL or AML as
            patient-derived xenografts (PDX) in mice</li>
        <li>biobank of re-transplantable PDX samples for&nbsp;<em>in vitro</em>&nbsp;studies, &gt;50
            for ALL and &gt;10 for AML</li>
        <li>genetic engineering in PDX (GEPDX) cells for overexpression of transgenes</li>
        <li>preclinical&nbsp;<em>in vivo</em>&nbsp;treatment trials monitored by
            bioluminescence&nbsp;<em>in vivo</em>&nbsp;imaging
            <ul>
                <li>quantifying treatment responses as early as 4 days after treatment</li>
                <li>covering all disease stages including minimal residual disease</li>
                <li>focusing on challenging sub-populations such as dormant stem cells</li>
            </ul>
        </li>
    </ul>

    <p><a href="https://www.helmholtz-munich.de/en/ahs" target="_blank">More information on the
            Xenograft Facility M&uuml;nchen</a></p>

    <h3>Leukemia Genomics Facility</h3>

    <div class="imagemodule float-left col-md-6">
        <div class="inner">
            <figure class="image"><span class="imagewrapper"><img alt="" data-id="0"
                        src="https://dktk.dkfz.de/download_file/view/b4765156-a61c-49bd-9677-8fc5ac3c93c6" /></span>

                <figcaption class="smalltext"><span class="copyright"><span class="copysign">&copy;</span> angellodeco /
                        Fotolia.com</span>
                </figcaption>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>

    <p><strong>Genetic characterisation plays a central role for diagnostics, treatment selection
            and prognostication in patients with leukemia. In close cooperation with local and DKTK
            partners, we support DKTK scientists exploring the biology and pathogenesis of
            leukemia.</strong></p>

    <p>Our aim is to develop innovative techniques for leukemia diagnostics. Our location at
        the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.klinikum.uni-muenchen.de/de/das_klinikum/index.html"
            target="_blank">Ludwig-Maximilians-Universit&auml;t M&uuml;nchen (LMU)&nbsp;</a>in
        the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.klinikum.uni-muenchen.de/Medizinische-Klinik-und-Poliklinik-III/de/index.html"
            target="_blank">Department of Hematology and Oncology</a>&nbsp;provides a close
        collaboration with the clinic and translational research groups. We have access to a large
        number of well-annotated patient specimens that have been collected at our routine
        diagnostics laboratory&nbsp;<a
            href="http://www.klinikum.uni-muenchen.de/Medizinische-Klinik-und-Poliklinik-III/de/leukaemiediagnostik/index.html"
            target="_blank">(Laboratory for Leukemia Diagnostics)</a>.</p>

    <p>&nbsp;</p>



    <div class="imagemodule float-right col-md-3">
        <div class="inner">
            <figure class="image"><span class="imagewrapper"><img alt="" data-id="0"
                        src="https://dktk.dkfz.de/download_file/view/3404ac44-2c0f-4311-abea-808ea668d83a/926" /></span>

                        sequencer</span> <span class="copyright"><span class="copysign">
                            &copy;</span> AG Spiekermann</span></figcaption>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <p>Our facility can provide the following services to DKTK-affiliated groups:</p>
    <p>Genetic characterization of human tissue specimens (primarily peripheral blood and bone
        marrow) using a variety of approaches including</p>
    <ul class="dk-custom-list">
        <li>Metaphase and molecular cytogenetics</li>
        <li>Conventional molecular genetics (Sanger sequencing, fragment analysis and qPCR; various
            assays established for recurrent mutations in hematopoietic neoplasms)</li>
        <li>Targeted next-generation sequencing (established panels of recurrently mutated genes in
            myeloid and lymphoid neoplasms)</li>
        <li>Whole-exome and whole-genome sequencing</li>
        <li>Single-cell genotyping</li>
    </ul>
    <p>&nbsp;</p>


    <div class="imagemodule float-right col-md-3">
        <div class="inner">

    <p>Our main areas of research focus on the following topics</p>
    <ul class="dk-custom-list">
        <li>Genetic characterisation of myeloid neoplasias</li>
        <li>Evaluation of novel prognostic and predictive markers in leukemia</li>
        <li>New therapeutic targets in leukemias</li>
        <li>Identification of markers for minimal residual disease (MRD)</li>
        <li>Research on clonal evolution of leukemia</li>
    </ul>

    <h3><br />Immune Analytics</h3>
    <div class="imagemodule float-right col-md-6">
        <div class="inner">
            <figure class="image"><span class="imagewrapper"><img alt="Labor Krackhardt.jpg" data-id="880"
                        src="https://dktk.dkfz.de/download_file/view_inline/42a3407e-8e5c-4c97-bf6c-8671c2df06b1" /></span>
                <figcaption class="smalltext"><span class="copyright"><span class="copysign">&copy;</span> A. Krackhardt
                        / Klinikum rechts der Isar
                        M&uuml;nchen</span></figcaption>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <p><strong>The Core Facility Immune Analytics at the DKTK partner site Munich has a broad
            expertise in identification and characterization of tumor-specific immune responses and
            offer support in a variety of in-depth analyses in tumor immunology.</strong></p>
    <p>Activities supported by the faciltiy include logistics and sampling of fresh tumor material
        and blood, preparation of tumor and blood material, phenotyping of PBMC and cells of the
        tumor microenvirnoment by flos cytometry using established panelds, cultivation and
        expansion fo tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes, establishment of tumor cell lines and
        EBV-transformed B cells, identification of tumor antigens, immunoprecipitation of MHC class
        I and II peptide complexes, isolation and genetic transfer of T cell receptors, sorting of
        single cells of the tumor microenvironment by flow cytometry, preparation of cell
        suspensions and signle cell solutions for sequencing, establishment of xenogeneic mouse
        models, immunoimaging, clinical correlation, support and counseling in preparation of
        clinical trials of immunotherapeutic approaches in cancer. The cooperation typically bases
        on scientific collaboration.</p>

    <h3><br />
        Genomics Technology and Informatics Platform</h3>

    <p><strong>Genome research deploying next generation sequencing and other genomic technologies
            is a major driver of discoveries in life science. Profiling genomes, epigenomes or
            transcriptomes in organisms, tissues or single cells has become a standard. </strong>
    </p>

    <p>At the same time, a rapidly growing number of sequencing applications is transforming the way
        we perform basic research across life sciences, with high-throughput sequencing-based
        methods complementing - and in many cases entirely replacing - classic &quot;gene by
        gene&quot; based experimental approaches.</p>

    <p>The Genomic Technology Unit aims to address the increasing need for sequencing applications
        and analytical capabilities in biomedical research. We develop genomic tools and protocols,
        implement emerging sequencing applications as well as bioinformatics methods and pipelines
        in various research ares, including genomics, epigenetics, (epi)transcriptomics and
        functional genomics. We cover a broad spectrum of applications, ranging from sequencing
        based profiling of genomes, chromatin or transcriptomes over genome-scale genetic screening
        methodologies to numerous individualized sequencing and bioinformatics requests.</p>

    <h3>Real-Time Cell Metabolic Analysis // Seahorse // Agilent</h3>

    <p>The Seahorse XF HS Mini Analyzer (Agilent Biotechnologies) measures mitochondrial respiration
        (oxygen consumption rate OCR) and glycolysis (extracellular acidification ECAR) of living
        cells (cultured cells and ex vivo samples) in an 8 well plate in real time. OCR and ECAR are
        key indicators of oxidative phophorylation and glycolysis. The instrument also allows the
        addition of substances and can therefore also make statements about their utilization.</p>
